Section 0

Assumptions & operating logic

Here are the logic and assumptions behind the case — everything downstream follows from them.

1 · GTM motionGuidde runs a PLG-hybrid motion — low-friction adoption generates intent, and the revenue team converts the right users into pipeline, customers, and expansion.
2 · Product usage is a first-class signalQualification is anchored on a Product-Qualified Lead (PQL), alongside marketing/sales signals.
3 · Person ≠ opportunityLifecycle stage (where is this person) and deal stage (where is this opportunity) are separate-but-connected systems.
4 · Ownership spans BDR · AE · CSBDR qualifies & routes; AE owns the active deal; CS supports trial and owns post-sale + expansion.
5 · Hybrid deal creationA human makes the qualification call, then the system creates and standardizes the deal — "judgment by human, creation by system."
6 · Outbound is a secondary on-rampIt feeds the same qualification gate as PLG — one pipeline, two acquisition motions.
7 · HubSpot tier (built on Sales Hub Pro trial)Workflows, custom lifecycle stages, and rotate-owner are Sales Hub Pro features (used here). Native contact lead scoring and marketing-engagement signals require Marketing Hub, which wasn't part of this trial — so those are presented as design and rebuilt with product-activation equivalents. 2 · PQL definition & the two routes to SQL

A contact becomes a PQL when…

any in-product activation event fires:

First guidde created Guidde published Teammate invited ★

★ Teammate-invite is weighted highest — it signals collaboration, virality, and expansion.

Two routes into SQL

1 · Direct intent — a demo is booked, or the BDR qualifies the contact.

2 · Accumulated product intent — two or more activation events stack up (the buildable equivalent of "lead score ≥ 50").

A single event makes someone a PQL (worth watching); explicit intent or accumulation makes them an SQL (worth a rep's time).

Why PQL — not MQL — is the gate In a PLG-hybrid model an MQL (marketing engagement) is a far weaker signal than a PQL (someone actually using the product). Investing heavily in an MQL→PQL hand-off risks turning MQL into a vanity metric. So MQL is kept lightweight and PQL is the real qualification gate — the SQL routes (product accumulation OR BDR/demo intent) are what earn a rep's time. An alternative worth discussing: collapsing to a single "Intent-Qualified Lead" — trial started OR demo requested — which also covers outbound leads who talk before they trial.
Implementation dependency — how product signals reach HubSpot A clean PQL is only as good as the data plumbing behind it. Activation events (First Guidde Created, etc.) originate in the product, so they must be piped into HubSpot reliably — via a native integration, a CDP/event pipeline (e.g., Segment), or reverse-ETL from the warehouse. SaaS orgs are notorious for weak product-analytics↔CRM integration and CRM hygiene, so this data architecture is part of the implementation plan, not an afterthought.

Two implementation notes on the pipeline Stage weights shown are illustrative defaults — in a real build they'd be calibrated to Guidde's historic stage-level win rates (if, say, 50% of deals reaching Evaluation close, that weight rises above 45%). Deal creation vs. "Opportunity": the deal is created at the SQL stage, but the contact's lifecycle flips to Opportunity only when the deal advances SQL → Discovery — so "Opportunity" means a deal actively being worked, not merely a record that exists.

A contact-based workflow that creates or updates an associated deal when a contact becomes sales-ready — "judgment by human, creation by system." Its hardest job is de-duplication: never open a second deal for a contact who already has one open.

DEDUPE — THE BUILD-READY DETAIL

Create vs. Update

The workflow branches on a Count of Open Deals rollup on the contact:

= 0 → Create a new deal at SQL, set Deal Source, owner = Contact Owner, auto-stamp ICP / Use Case / Product Interest / Amount / Close Date, create a 48h task.

≥ 1 → Update the open deal: Demo → Discovery; Trial Started → Evaluation; refresh activity.

VALIDATION & ACCURACY

Guardrails on the data

• Missing required field → "Incomplete deal" task + hold.
• Idle beyond stage SLA → stale-deal alert.
• Closed Lost → require structured Closed-Lost Reason + Competitor.
• Closed Won → auto-trigger the CS handoff.

2-B · Deal creation

Who & when

The BDR makes the qualification call (ICP fit + product behavior + use-case clarity + buying group + urgency). HubSpot then creates the deal automatically — no missed opportunities, no pipeline flooding.

Mandatory at creation

Deal Source · Primary Contact · ICP Fit · Use Case · Amount/ACV · Buying Group · Timeline · Product Interest.

Validation

No duplicate open deals (rollup); Closed-Lost Reason is a picklist; required fields increase by stage; incomplete records block progression.

2-C · Risks, bottlenecks & tooling

⚠

Operational risk

PLG-hybrid is fragile if qualification is loose — over-routing wastes rep time, under-routing misses intent. Mitigation: explicit activation rules + SLA monitoring.

⌛

The bottleneck

The Trial/POC → commercial transition. Prospects can use Guidde without revealing buying process. Evaluation must be a structured checkpoint — success criteria, active-user counts, CS onboarding.

🛠

Tooling

Enrichment (Clearbit/Apollo) for ICP firmographics; dedup/data-quality monitor (HubSpot Data Quality / Insycle); SLA alerting in workflows.
