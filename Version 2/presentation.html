Assumptions

The logic this is all built on

Everything downstream follows from these.

1 · GTM motionGuidde runs a PLG-hybrid motion — adoption generates intent; the revenue team converts the right users into pipeline and expansion.
2 · Product usage is a first-class signalQualification is anchored on a Product-Qualified Lead (PQL), alongside marketing and sales signals.
3 · Person ≠ opportunityLifecycle stage (the person) and deal stage (the opportunity) are separate-but-connected systems.
4 · Ownership spans BDR · AE · CSBDR qualifies and routes; AE owns the deal; CS supports trial and owns post-sale.
5 · Hybrid deal creationA human makes the qualification call, then the system creates and standardizes the deal.
6 · Outbound is a secondary on-rampIt feeds the same qualification gate as PLG — one pipeline, two acquisition motions.
7 · HubSpot tierBuilt on a Sales Hub Professional trial. Native lead scoring and marketing-engagement signals need Marketing Hub (documented as design); the live build uses product-activation equivalents.

Create vs. Update

Before creating anything, the workflow checks whether the contact already has an associated deal:

none → Create a new deal at SQL, owner = Contact Owner, set Deal Source & Amount, associate it to the contact, create a 48h task, notify the owner.

has one → Update the existing deal — in the full design: Demo → Discovery, Trial → Evaluation; built here as a review task.

7 · Tier & scope

What's built vs documented

Built on a Sales Hub Professional trial; Marketing Hub was not available. The honest map:

✓ Built on Sales Hub — what's live

  • Workflows (both automations)
  • Custom lifecycle stage (PQL) between MQL and SQL
  • Product-activation triggers — First Guidde / Published / Teammate Invited
  • BDR-qualified property & routing
  • Rotate-owner & internal notifications
  • Forms & product-signup property (scoped out, not blocked)
  • Native lifecycle sync (deal-created → Opportunity, deal-won → Customer)

Workflow 1 — documented as design (needs Marketing Hub)

  • Native lead scoring (hubspotscore is read-only; set only by the Marketing Hub scoring app)
  • Marketing-email click tracking (the "≥2 clicks" MQL signal)
  • Page-view / pricing-page behavior tracking
The workaroundWorkflow 1 reads the underlying product-activation events directly instead of a score — same signal, same outcome. With a full Marketing + Sales stack, the point model is configured in Settings → Lead Scoring and the branch simply checks HubSpot Score ≥ 50.

Workflow 2 — documented as design (needs Operations Hub or a separate workflow)

  • Open-deals de-dupe rollup — needs Operations Hub; the build uses HubSpot's native associated-deal count
  • Full deal-stage progression (Demo → Discovery, Trial → Evaluation) — belongs in a deal-based workflow
  • Validation guardrails — incomplete-deal hold, stale-deal alert, CS handoff on won
  • Live Amount lookup (plan × seats / CPQ) — the build uses a static demo default
The one-line framingEarly-funnel triggers (form fills, marketing engagement, signup) span Sales + Marketing Hub, so some are only partially buildable on a Sales Hub trial. I focused the live build on the product-qualification mechanism (PQL → SQL) — the core of a PLG-hybrid model and fully buildable here. The complete lifecycle is detailed in the written design.

2 · Deal creation

Judgment by human, creation by system

Who & when

The BDR makes the qualification call (ICP fit + product behavior + use-case clarity + buying group + urgency). HubSpot then creates the deal automatically — no missed opportunities from forgotten manual creation, no pipeline flooding.

Mandatory at creation

Deal Source · Primary Contact · ICP Fit · Use Case · Amount/ACV · Buying Group · Timeline · Product Interest. Covers the assignment's named examples.

Validation rules

No duplicate open deals per contact; Closed-Lost Reason is a picklist, never free text; required fields increase by stage; incomplete records block progression.

Supports forecasting: consistent creation + dedupe + advancement → trustworthy stage-conversion, weighted pipeline, and close dates.

3 · Risks, bottlenecks & tooling

What could break — and how to harden it

⚠

Operational risk

PLG-hybrid is fragile if qualification is loose — over-routing wastes rep time, under-routing misses intent. Mitigation: explicit activation rules, the score threshold, and SLA monitoring on follow-up speed.

⌛

The bottleneck

The Trial / POC → commercial transition. Prospects can use Guidde without revealing buying process. Evaluation must be a structured checkpoint — success criteria, active-user counts, CS onboarding.

🛠

Tooling

Enrichment (Clearbit / Apollo) for ICP firmographics; a dedup / data-quality monitor (HubSpot Data Quality or Insycle); SLA alerting in workflows.

1 · KPI framework

Built around the four questions leadership actually asks

A headline KPI strip up top, then four panels — each owning one leadership question:

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The at-a-glance snapshot strip

Open pipeline · Commit / Best-case forecast · Closed Won (QTD) · Avg sales cycle — the header before drilling into the four panels.

PIPELINE & FORECASTING · Q1

Where are the bottlenecks — will we hit the number?

Pipeline by stage, coverage vs target, stage-to-stage conversion, weighted pipeline by quarter, forecast accuracy, avg days in stage.

CHANNEL PERFORMANCE · Q2

Which channels drive revenue?

PQLs, Won $ & win rate by acquisition channel — where to invest, read as volume × quality per channel.

SALES PERFORMANCE · Q3

Who is performing best?

Won $, quota attainment, win rate & SQL→Won by rep — surfaces coaching needs and quota risk early.

PRODUCTIVITY & EFFICIENCY · Q4

What will increase revenue?

SLA attainment, funnel-conversion gaps, loss-reason patterns, won-handoff completion — points to the fixable leaks.

Quota vs. Target & feasibilityQuota = an individual rep's number; Target = the team's collective goal — kept distinct. Most KPIs build natively in HubSpot reports; a few polished cuts (weighted pipeline, forecast accuracy) sit in a BI layer fed by HubSpot.
